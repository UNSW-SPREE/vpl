Mid-session Assessment
(Diffusion and Silver Screen-printing)

This assessment task is worth 10% of the marks in this course.  It focuses on optimising the diffusion conditions in conjunction with the formation of the front metal grid using the screen-printing of silver paste followed by high temperature firing to facilitate ohmic contact between the Ag and the n-type Si.  These are probably the most important two processes to optimise and the two with greatest interdependence. 

Experience in optimising the diffusion conditions was gained during Assignment 4, although reoptimisation will be necessary for this assessment task as the silver firing conditions are altered. The silver screen-printing process can be split into two, a printing step followed by the drying and firing process in the furnace.   For the printing process, the user controllable parameters include the strand diameter of the mesh for the screen, the number of strands/cm for the screen mesh, the emulsion thicknesses for the top and bottom surfaces of the screen that define the pattern, the finger spacing, the paste viscosity and the squeegee pressure and speed.  The drying/firing process is carried out in a belt furnace, with the first section of the furnace being the drying zone to ensure the paste is dry.  The processing variables to be user specified for the firing process are : belt speed (which affects both the drying and the metal firing time); drying temperature; and the Zone 1, Zone 2 and Zone 3 temperatures. Zone 1 and Zone 3 temperatures should be selected to be approximately 150 degrees below the Zone 2 temperature so as to help maintain a uniform temperature profile across the Zone 2. These two zones should also be significantly shorter in length than the Zone 2.   All other processing parameters for all processes other than the diffusion step and the screen-printing/firing of the silver remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.

This assessment task will take a similar form to previous assignments except that you will also be required to write a 2-page report that discusses the importance of the various processing parameters and their interdependencies. The assignment batch will again contain 10 wafers. You may process as many batches as you like while trying to optimise the processing parameters of the “diffusion conditions” in conjunction with the “silver screening and firing”.  Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn more about the significance of the diffusion and silver screen-printing/firing parameters and their optimisation.  There are a large range of tests you can perform on partially processed and finished cells to help diagnose any weaknesses. An important aim in optimising the processing conditions for the silver printing/firing is to produce a final IV curve that as closely as possible approximates the pseudo IV curve from prior to the silver contact application.  This involves minimising the series resistive losses (Rs) associated with extracting the generated current from the solar cell but while simultaneously trying to minimise shading losses. The shading losses can be observed through the reflection test while insight into the Rs losses can be gained through the IV curve, the pseudo IV curve, the series resistance, the silver paste thickness (before and after firing) and the silver resistance test. You may process multiple “Assignment batches”, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy Task (i) and may submit the results from a separate batch to satisfy the requirements of Task (ii).   Each task is worth 25% of the marks, with the efficiencies achieved directly determining the marks awarded.  The written report determines the other 50% of the marks.

Assessable Tasks

(ii) Produce an Assignment batch with the highest single cell efficiency.  (25% of marks)

Part B (written report)

Write a report of approximately 2 pages length that discusses the importance of the various processing parameters and their interdependencies for the diffusion and silver screen-printing/firing steps.  Make particular reference to how and why these impact the performance and/or yields of the solar cells being made.

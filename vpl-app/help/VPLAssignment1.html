Assignment 1 (Saw Damage Removal Etch)

Assignment 1 familiarises the user with the first process in manufacturing screen-printed silicon solar cells. The wafers must be etched to remove the residual surface damage and contaminants that result from sawing the ingot into wafers. The temperature, sodium hydroxide concentration of the etching solution, and the time in the chemical bath may be specified by the user. All other processing parameters for all other processes throughout the production line remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.

The assignment batch contains 10 wafers. You may process as many batches as you like while trying to optimise the processing parameters of the “saw damage removal etch“.  Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn about the significance of the etching parameters and their optimisation.  There are also various tests you can perform on finished and partially finished cells to help monitor performance during processing. You may process multiple “Assignment batches“, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy task A and may submit the results from a separate batch to satisfy the requirements of Task B.   Often in manufacturing, we find that the batch that produces the highest efficiency cell (as required for task A, does not necessarily produce the highest average cell efficiency for the whole batch (as required for task B).  Each task is worth 50% of the marks, with the efficiencies achieved directly determining the marks awarded:

Assessable Tasks

Task A:    To produce an Assignment batch with the highest efficiency single cell. The quality of the remainder of the cells does not matter.  (note: The higher the efficiency the higher the mark)

Task B:      To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average cell efficiency over the 10 cells. (Note that efficiency of broken cells is 0).

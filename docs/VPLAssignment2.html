Assignment 2 (Texturing)

Assignment 2 familiarises the user with the texturing process in manufacturing silicon solar cells. Texturing is designed to decrease the amount of light reflected from the solar cell's surface by producing pyramids that cause incident light to be multiply reflected before having a chance to escape.  Pyramid formation is most effective with a particular crystal orientation for the silicon known as (100) orientation.  It is therefore far less effective with multicrystalline silicon wafers than single crystal wafers. The temperature, sodium hydroxide concentration, propanol concentration, exhaust flow rate and the time in the chemical bath are the processing parameters that may be specified by the user. The assignment batch again contains 10 wafers. All other processing parameters for all other processes throughout the production line must again remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.

Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn about the significance of the texturing parameters and their optimisation.  There are also various tests you can perform on finished and partially finished cells to help monitor performance during processing. You may process multiple “Assignment batches“, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy task A and may submit the results from separate batches to satisfy the requirements of Tasks B & C.   Often in manufacturing, we find that the batch that produces the highest efficiency cell (as required for task A, does not necessarily produce the highest average cell efficiency for the whole batch (as required for task B).  Each task is worth one third of the marks, with the efficiencies achieved and pyramid coverage area directly determining the marks awarded:

Assessable Tasks

Task A:    To produce an Assignment batch with the highest efficiency single cell. The quality of the remainder of the cells does not matter.  (note: The higher the cell efficiency the higher the mark)

Task B:    To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average cell efficiency over the 10 cells. (Note that efficiency of broken cells is 0).

Task C:    To produce a cell with the highest percentage of the cell surface covered with pyramids (test via the “pyramid coverage visual inspection test“)

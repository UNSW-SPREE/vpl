Assignment 8 (Anti-reflection Coating)

Assignment 8 familiarises the user with the formation of an anti-reflection (AR) coating in the manufacturing of silicon solar cells. AR coatings are designed to decrease the amount of light reflected from the solar cell's surface by causing destructive interference for the light that would otherwise be reflected from the silicon surface.  The use of such coatings can have a major impact on cells that are not textured such as those made from multicrystalline silicon.  In these cases, AR coatings are essential.  Providing the deposition temperature is within an appropriate range, the gas flow for the deposition chamber and the furnace belt speed are the main two processing parameters that may be specified by the user that will impact the thickness of the AR coating.

Another important benefit of the AR coating is that it can be used to restrict the distance the silver paste penetrates into the silicon.  This can be of benefit because the phosphorus concentration within the silicon is normally higher towards the surface of the silicon therefore facilitating lower contact resistance through restricting the penetration depth of the metal.  This also facilitates the use of shallower diffused junctions which may have benefits in reducing the surface dead layer.  The thickness of the AR coating needs to be optimised for reducing surface reflection although independently of this, varying the deposition temperature will vary the hardness of the layer which in turn can be used to affect how easily the metal penetrates through and therefore how far it penetrates into the silicon.  There are tests that enable you to measure the surface reflection and hence determine the effectiveness of the AR coating at reducing reflection.  However, the measurement of the Jsc is probably the most useful indicator since this is directly proportional to the amount of light entering the cell.

The assignment batch again contains 10 wafers. All other processing parameters for all other processes throughout the production line must again remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.  Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn about the significance of the AR coating and its deposition parameters  and their optimisation.  There are also various tests you can perform on finished and partially finished cells to help monitor the effectiveness of the processing. You may process multiple “Assignment batches”, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy task A and may submit the results from a separate batch to satisfy the requirements of Task B.   Often in manufacturing, we find that the batch that produces the highest efficiency cell (as required for task A, does not necessarily produce the highest average cell efficiency for the whole batch (as required for task B).  Each task is worth equal marks, with the efficiencies achieved directly determining the marks awarded:

Assessable Tasks

Task A:     To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average short circuit current density.

Task B:    To produce an Assignment batch with the highest efficiency single cell. The quality of the remainder of the cells does not matter.  (note: The higher the cell efficiency the higher the mark)

Task C:     To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average cell efficiency over the 10 cells. (Note that efficiency of broken cells is 0).

Study Questions

1. Why does the optimisation of the AR coating deposition conditions to produce the highest single screen-printed cell efficiency lead to poor yields?

2. Why can the inclusion of an AR coating help both the series resistance and also perhaps the cell short wavelength response?

3. Will the silver screen-printing and firing processing parameters have to be reoptimised each time AR coating deposition conditions are changed?

4. Should the AR coating conditions be reoptimised each time the diffusion conditions are changed?

5. What are the consequences of poor uniformity in the AR coating thickness across the wafer?

6. What problems may occur if there are pin-holes in the AR coating layer?

7. Why are AR coatings more important for multicrystalline silicon solar cells?

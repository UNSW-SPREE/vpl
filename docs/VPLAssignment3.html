Assignment 3 (Acid clean)

Assignment 3 familiarises the user with the cleaning processes that follow the etching and texturing of the silicon.  The HF treatment removes any thin oxide layers that may have formed on the silicon and more importantly, any residual sodium silicate that may remain on the surface.  This treatment is followed by a rinsing process and then a treatment in HCl to remove any metal atoms that would otherwise potentially damage the minority carrier diffusion lengths following high temperature exposure. The processing variables to be user specified for each acid treatment are the concentration of the acid, the length of time the wafers are left in the acid and the subsequent rinsing time in high purity water. All other processing parameters for all other processes throughout the production line remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.

The assignment batch contains 10 wafers. You may process as many batches as you like while trying to optimise the processing parameters of the “acid clean“.  Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn about the significance of these cleaning parameters and their optimisation.  There are also various tests you can perform on finished and partially finished cells to help monitor performed during processing. You may process multiple “Assignment batches“, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy task A and may submit the results from a separate batch to satisfy the requirements of Task B.   Often in manufacturing, we find that the batch that produces the highest efficiency cell (as required for task A), does not necessarily produce the highest average cell efficiency for the whole batch (as required for task B).  Each task is worth 50% of the marks, with the efficiencies achieved directly determining the marks awarded:

Assessable Tasks (both tasks are of equal value)

Task A:    To produce an Assignment batch with the highest efficiency single cell. The quality of the remainder of the cells does not matter.  (note: The higher the efficiency the higher the mark)

Task B:      To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average cell efficiency over the 10 cells. (Note that efficiency of broken cells is 0).

Assignment 6 (Edge Junction Isolation by Plasma Etching)

Assignment 6 focuses on the removal of the phosphorus diffused region down the edges of the wafer that cause shunting between the front and rear metal contacts.  These diffused edges are normally removed by plasma etching with the primary processing parameters being the plasma power and the duration of the etching process  (assuming an appropriate gas and corresponding flow rate is maintained).  One problem with plasma based processes is that the high energy ions tend to damage the exposed silicon material which can be quite critical in this case due to the junction potentially being exposed to the plasma.  All other processing parameters for all other processes throughout the production line remain fixed at the default values and therefore cannot be varied for this assignment.

The assignment batch contains 10 wafers. You may process as many batches as you like while trying to optimise the processing parameters for the “plasma etching“.  Your overall aim is to achieve the highest performance cells but while simultaneously achieving good yields.  There are help files that can be accessed to help you understand and learn about the significance of these plasma etching parameters and their optimisation.  There are also various tests you can perform on finished and partially finished cells to help monitor performance during processing. You may process multiple “Assignment batches“, in order to achieve the best possible results.  You will submit the results from one batch to satisfy Task A and may submit the results from separate batches to satisfy the requirements of the other Tasks.  Each task is worth 25% of the marks, with the efficiencies/lifetimes achieved directly determining the marks awarded:

Assessable Tasks (all tasks are of equal value)

Task A: To produce the cell with the highest shunt resistance

Task B: To produce the cell with the highest pseudo efficiency on the pseudo IV curve (which can be measured directly following the plasma etching process)

Task C:  To achieve the highest open circuit voltage from the pseudo IV curve for a single cell.

Task D: To produce an Assignment batch with the highest average cell efficiency over the 10 cells. (Note that efficiency of broken cells is 0).

Study Questions

1. For the cell with the highest pseudo efficiency, why does this not also have the highest open circuit voltage as determined in Task B?

2. Why is it that virtually the same conditions of plasma etching are required to optimise both the pseudo efficiency and the real cell efficiency (which is very unlike the cases for both Al firing and phosphorus diffusion)?

3. Would the impact of these edge effects get better or worse if we made the cells bigger?

4. Why doesn’t the achievement of the highest shunt resistance imply we have achieved the best edge junction isolation and therefore give us the highest efficiency solar cell?
